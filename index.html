PostEx Tracking: Check Your Parcel Status Online in Pakistan

Waiting for a parcel can be stressful. You may keep thinking, “Where is my order?” or “When will it reach my home?” If your parcel was sent through PostEx, you can check its latest status online with PostEx tracking.

This easy guide explains how to track a PostEx parcel, where to find your PostEx tracking number, what each delivery status means, and what to do if your order is late.

What is PostEx tracking?

PostEx tracking is an online system that helps you check your parcel status. Every parcel gets a special tracking number. This number is also called a tracking ID, order ID, consignment number, or reference number.

When you enter this number in the tracking box, you can see the latest update about your parcel. You may see if your order is booked, picked up, in transit, out for delivery, delivered, on hold, or returned.

PostEx courier tracking is helpful for online shopping orders, COD parcels, Instagram store orders, Daraz seller orders, and business shipments across Pakistan.

Where can you find your PostEx tracking number?

To check your PostEx parcel tracking, you need your tracking number first. You can usually find it in the SMS, WhatsApp message, order email, parcel receipt, seller message, or online store order page.

The PostEx tracking number is usually a long number. Sometimes it may also include letters. Always copy it carefully because one wrong digit can show no result.

How to track your PostEx parcel online

You can check your PostEx order tracking in a few simple steps. Enter your PostEx tracking number in the search box at the top of this page. Remove extra spaces before or after the number. Then click the Track Now button and wait for the latest parcel status.

You do not need to create an account for normal tracking. Your PostEx tracking ID is enough to check the delivery status. If your parcel was booked just now, wait 12 to 24 hours because new shipments can take time to appear in the system.

What details can you see in PostEx tracking?

When you track your PostEx parcel, you may see the current parcel status, last shipment update, booking date, pickup information, delivery progress, city or hub update, out for delivery update, delivered status, or returned status.

Some results may also show a tracking history. This helps you understand the full journey of your parcel from booking to delivery.

Common PostEx tracking status meanings

PostEx tracking status can sometimes be confusing. Here are the simple meanings of common updates.

Booked

This means your order has been added to the PostEx system. The parcel may still be with the seller or waiting for pickup.

Picked Up

This means the PostEx rider or team has collected the parcel from the seller.

In Transit

This means your parcel is moving from one city, hub, or warehouse to another. This status is common for intercity parcels.

Arrived at Hub

This means your parcel has reached a PostEx sorting center or delivery hub.

Out for Delivery

This means the rider has your parcel and may deliver it today. Keep your phone active because the rider may call you.

Delivered

This means the parcel has reached the customer successfully.

On Hold

This means there is a small issue with delivery. It may happen because of an incomplete address, customer not answering the call, payment issue, or delivery delay.

Returned to Shipper

This means the parcel is being sent back to the seller. This can happen if delivery failed, the customer refused the parcel, or the address was not correct.

How long does PostEx delivery take?

PostEx delivery time depends on the city, distance, seller dispatch time, and parcel load. In the same city, delivery may take around 1 to 2 working days after pickup. For another city, delivery may take around 2 to 5 working days.

During Eid, sale days, bad weather, or high order load, delivery can take a little longer. These are general timeframes only. The exact delivery time can change based on your parcel route and courier process.

Why is my PostEx tracking not updating?

Sometimes PostEx tracking does not update for many hours. This does not always mean your parcel is lost. Tracking can stop updating when the parcel is moving between cities, waiting for the next scan, or still not picked up from the seller.

It can also happen because of high parcel load, delayed system updates, or pending rider and hub scans. If your PostEx parcel status does not change for more than 2 to 3 working days, contact the seller first. If needed, contact PostEx support with your tracking number.

What to do if your PostEx parcel is late

If your PostEx shipment tracking shows no new update, first check your tracking number again. Make sure there are no spaces or typing mistakes. Then wait 12 to 24 hours after booking.

If the parcel is still late, contact the seller and ask if the parcel was picked up. You can also contact PostEx support if the delay is too long. Keep your phone active because the rider may call before delivery.

Tips for easy PostEx parcel tracking

Save your PostEx tracking ID in your phone notes. Copy the tracking number instead of typing it manually. Check the status once or twice a day. Do not refresh again and again after every few minutes.

Contact the seller if the parcel is not picked up. Contact support if the parcel is stuck for many working days. Also make sure your address and phone number are correct when ordering.

PostEx tracking for COD orders

Many online stores in Pakistan use PostEx for cash on delivery orders. If you ordered a COD parcel, you can still track it with your PostEx tracking number.

When the status shows “Out for Delivery,” keep the payment ready. Also keep your phone near you. If the rider cannot contact you, delivery may be delayed or marked as failed.

Can you track PostEx without an account?

Yes, you can track a PostEx parcel without an account. You only need the tracking number. Enter it in the tracking box and check the latest order status.

A seller or business account may show more details, but normal customers can still check basic parcel updates online.

PostEx tracking makes it easy to check your parcel status online. You only need your PostEx tracking number. Enter it in the tracking tool and you can see the latest delivery update.

If your parcel is not updating, wait for some time. Many parcels update after reaching the next hub. If there is no update for more than 2 to 3 working days, contact the seller or PostEx support.

PostEx tracking FAQs

How can I track my PostEx parcel?

You can track your PostEx parcel by entering your tracking number in the search field above. After clicking Track Now, you will see the latest parcel status and tracking history when available.

Where can I find my PostEx tracking number?

You can find it in the SMS, WhatsApp message, order email, seller message, receipt, or online store order page.

Why is my PostEx tracking not updating?

It may happen when your parcel is moving between cities, waiting for the next scan, or the system update is delayed. Wait for some time and check again.

What does Out for Delivery mean in PostEx tracking?

It means the rider has your parcel and may deliver it today. Keep your phone active and payment ready if it is a COD order.

What does On Hold mean in PostEx tracking?

It means there may be a delivery issue. The issue can be a wrong address, no response from the customer, payment problem, or delay at the delivery hub.

How long does PostEx delivery take?

Same-city delivery may take 1 to 2 working days. Intercity delivery may take 2 to 5 working days. Delivery time can change during busy days.

Can I track a PostEx COD parcel?

Yes. COD parcels can be tracked with the same PostEx tracking number shared by the seller.

What should I do if my PostEx parcel is delayed?

First, check your tracking number. Then contact the seller. If the parcel is still stuck, contact PostEx support with your tracking ID.

Can I track a PostEx parcel without login?

Yes, normal parcel tracking does not need login. You only need your PostEx tracking number.

Why does PostEx show Returned to Shipper?

This means the parcel is going back to the seller. It can happen due to failed delivery, wrong address, customer refusal, or no contact with the customer.
