Tracking Status Guide

PostEx tracking status meanings

Use this page when your parcel is moving but the wording is confusing. The exact wording can vary, but these explanations cover the common PostEx tracking stages shown in shipment history.

StatusMeaningWhat to do
At seller warehouseThe shipment has been created or packed by the seller.Wait for pickup or first PostEx scan.
Received at warehousePostEx has received the parcel at a warehouse or hub.Track again after the next route update.
DepartedThe parcel has left one facility for another city, hub, or destination.No action needed unless it stays unchanged for several days.
Arrived at transit hubThe parcel reached an intermediate sorting or route point.Wait for the delivery-city or final-mile scan.
Waiting for deliveryThe parcel is ready or pending for a delivery attempt.Keep phone available for rider contact.
Enroute for deliveryThe shipment is moving toward customer delivery.Keep COD amount ready if applicable.
Delivered to customerThe parcel has been marked delivered.Confirm receipt with household, office, or reception if needed.
ReturnedThe shipment may be moving back to the seller.Contact the seller with your tracking number.

Why status history matters

A single current status can hide the real parcel journey. The timeline on the PostEx tracking homepage shows the latest API history points in order, which helps you understand whether the shipment is moving normally, waiting at a hub, out for delivery, or already delivered. If your question is location-based, open the PostEx office near me page. If your parcel is COD, the COD guide explains payment-related expectations.
