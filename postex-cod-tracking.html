Cash on Delivery

PostEx COD tracking guide

Cash on delivery parcels need clear communication because the customer, seller, rider, and payment cycle are all connected. Use your tracking number on the homepage to see where the parcel is before arranging payment or contacting the seller.

1. Seller booking

The seller creates the shipment and prepares the parcel for pickup. Tracking may not show full movement immediately.

2. PostEx pickup

The parcel enters the PostEx network and receives warehouse or route scans as it moves.

3. Delivery and COD

The rider attempts delivery, collects cash if applicable, and the parcel is marked delivered after successful handover.

Tips for COD customers and sellers

Customers should keep the payable amount ready when the status becomes enroute for delivery. Sellers should share the correct tracking number with customers and use the PostEx tracking page before responding to delivery complaints. If the parcel is delayed, read the status meanings to understand whether it is still in transit or waiting for delivery. For city-level help, use the office near me page.
